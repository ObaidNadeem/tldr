I've been quietly shipping shader-driven sites since 2021, back when putting WebGL on a landing page got you weird looks. Most sites were still doing CSS fades and maybe a scroll library if they were feeling brave, and anything on the GPU read as showing off. Not anymore. Now every launch page wants a shader.

A shader is a tiny program that runs on your GPU, once per pixel, thousands of pixels at a time. That's how you get effects the DOM can't touch: fluid distortion, grain, light, all locked at sixty frames. The tradeoff is a different way of thinking, closer to painting with math than stacking divs.

Restraint is the flex

The best shader work is almost invisible. You feel it before you clock it. One signature effect beats ten decorative ones, every single time. A hero that reacts to your cursor says more than a page drowning in motion ever will, because control reads louder than enthusiasm.

This page is the demo, which felt like the honest way to do it. Every sentence was written once, with the skippable words marked right in the source.
